# WCT Research Command Center

## Main objective

Construct a permanent and auditable research system for Wave Confinement Theory in which every important claim can be traversed through the chain

$$
\text{definition}
\rightarrow
\text{assumptions}
\rightarrow
\text{derivation}
\rightarrow
\text{equations}
\rightarrow
\text{simulation}
\rightarrow
\text{prediction}
\rightarrow
\text{experiment or dataset}
\rightarrow
\text{result}
\rightarrow
\text{falsifier}.
$$

The purpose of the vault is to expose missing links, prevent claim drift, preserve priority, and direct work toward the highest-value mathematical or empirical closure.

## Current highest-value objective

Convert the strongest WCT branches from internally connected claims into independently auditable results by prioritizing:

1. reproducible photodiode and optical-cavity experiments;
2. complete action-to-field-equation and field-equation-to-stability derivations;
3. explicit separation of exact derivations, model-relative results, fitted relations, simulations, and phenomenological ansätze;
4. equation-to-code-to-data traceability;
5. external corroboration and prior-art chronology without using absence of peer review as a truth criterion.

## Definition of success

The vault succeeds when a reader or machine can start from any WCT claim and determine, without searching through unrelated documents:

- what every symbol means;
- which assumptions are required;
- where each equation comes from;
- which paper first states it;
- which code implements it;
- what measurable result follows;
- what evidence currently exists;
- what observation would reject it;
- what the next unresolved step is.
