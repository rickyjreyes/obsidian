> [!info] Classification

## Canonical statement

*Generates: $n \leq 3$, blow-up exclusion, curvature regularity, ψ-electron dimensional constraints.*

Sobolev embedding controls pointwise curvature in terms of $H^2$-regularity:

$$H^2(\Omega) \hookrightarrow L^\infty(\Omega) \text{ for bounded smooth } \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^n \implies n \leq 3$$

- For $d > 3$, $H^2$ does **not** control $L^\infty$ → curvature-locked fields may unwind.

- For $d \leq 3$, finite $\mathcal{E}_{\text{WCT}}$ and curvature locking imply **regular, confined $\psi$** fields.

- Combined with CL6 and CL10, this supports the conditional statement that **stable ψ-electron solitons are only realized in ≤3 spatial dimensions**.
